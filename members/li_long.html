Long Li, Assoc. Prof

p.yang@njnu.edu.cn  |  peiqiyangweb@gmail.com

Li Long, an instructor and specially appointed associate researcher, centers his research on thermal environment remote sensing, vegetation remote sensing, and global change. His focus includes investigating the influence of urban 3D structure and climate change on urban heat patterns, exploring thermal effects from forest disturbances, and analyzing vegetation change dynamics under land use management. He develops high spatiotemporal resolution data products for temperature and air temperature and studies post-wildfire vegetation recovery. He has demonstrated strong scholarly output, publishing 17 papers as first or corresponding author in prestigious journals such as Remote Sensing of Environment (RSE), Geophysical Research Letters (GRL), and Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (JGR-A), and actively serves as a reviewer for over 20 SCI journals.
